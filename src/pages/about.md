Most contractor software reviews are useless.

They're either written by someone who has never actually run a service business, or they're effectively vendor content — carefully worded to say nothing negative about any product that might be paying an affiliate commission. You end up reading four comparison articles and knowing less than when you started.

That's what ServiceToolBase is trying to fix.

I'm Chris Harper. I started this site after spending more time than I'd like to admit trying to find honest, specific information about contractor software — and consistently coming up empty. What I found instead was a lot of articles that listed the same six tools, described each one's features in language lifted directly from the vendor's own website, and concluded with a recommendation based on nothing.

## WHAT WE ACTUALLY DO HERE

We aggregate real feedback from contractors who use this software every day. Every review on ServiceToolBase follows the same process:

- We dig through G2, Capterra, Google Play, the App Store, Reddit, and trade forums to find what real contractors are saying — the good and the bad. Complaints that show up repeatedly across independent reviewers are real problems, regardless of what the vendor says.
- We dig into pricing honestly — including what's locked behind higher tiers, what onboarding typically costs, and what a realistic first-year spend looks like for a small contracting business.
- We synthesize patterns from hundreds of real user reviews to surface what actually matters: what works on the job site, what breaks, and what's not worth paying for.
- We call out real weaknesses. If users consistently report a clunky mobile app, thin reporting, or slow customer support — that's in the review.

## HOW WE MAKE MONEY

ServiceToolBase earns affiliate commissions when you click through to a software product and sign up. That's disclosed on every article and on our affiliate disclosure page.

This means we have a financial incentive to be helpful enough that you trust the recommendation — because if the reviews aren't useful, you don't click, and the whole thing falls apart. A bad tool gets a critical review here, regardless of commission rates.

## WHO THIS IS FOR

Painting contractors, electricians, plumbers, HVAC technicians, roofers, landscapers, general contractors — anyone running a real service business who needs to choose software without sitting through six vendor demos to get a straight answer.

Questions or feedback? Use the contact page. If you've used a tool we haven't reviewed, or you think we got something wrong, say so.
